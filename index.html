Cutting up Sounds in the Woods

25 words are chosen randomly in the poem Sounds in the Woods by Jack Kerouac. The original text is a mix of cryptic passages and various onomatopoeias meant to conjure up buzzing insects and other forest noises. Cutting up this abstract, sensory poem generates shorter poems with a similar feel.

The shorter poem is also used to draw a tree.
